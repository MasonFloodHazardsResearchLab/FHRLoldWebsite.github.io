iFLOOD: Chesapeake Bay Flood Forecast System powered by ADCIRC + NAM

× Warning! System is currently migrating to new portal. Please follow this link http://iflood.vse.gmu.edu/map/

Real-time water level time series forecasts for 84 hours are provided every 6 hours daily for the AHPS/NOAA monitoring stations.

Mason Forecast System results are presented in green color titled iFlood Forecast and are compared with NOAA official forecast models Extratropical Surge and Tide Operational Forecast System (ESTOFS) , Chesapeake Bay Operational Forecast System (CBOFS) and Extratropical Storm Surge Model (ETSS). ETSS and ESTOFS systems uses Global Forecast System (GFS) meteorological forcing to predict water levels. CBOFS is based on the National Weather Service (NWS)/Real Time Mesoscale Analysis (RTMA) winds (for the nowcast) and an interpolation of the NWS North American Mesoscale (NAM)-12 atmospheric forecast model data.

National Weather Service (NWS) official forecast is presented in red color labeled AHPS and is also being validated with observed data from NOAA.
